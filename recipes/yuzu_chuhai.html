Yuzu Chuhai (Japanese)

What is the Yuzu Chuhai?

Chuhai is a cocktail containing the Japanese alcohol called shochu, soda, and any kind of fruit juice. In Tokyo area we sometimes call it Sour instead of Chuhai. This cocktail is very popular in Japan, commonly served in izakaya-style restaurants and restaurants that cater to Japanese salary workers. This Chuhai contains yuzu juice, a juice made from the yuzu citrus fruit. Valued for its aromatic and flavorful juice and rind, it is commonly used in many aspects of Japanese cuisine.

The History of the Chuhai

It's believed that the Chuhai originated in Eastern Tokyo after the war, most likely in the Hikifune neighborhood of Shumida City in Tokyo. Sanyu Sakabar near the Keisei Hikifune Station claims to be the birthplace. Still around today, the Chuhai rivalry in the neighborhood remains strong with several shops making the same claim.

Ingredients:

(makes 1 yuzu chuhai)

  • 1/2 cup club soda
  • 1/4 cup shochu
  • 1 tbsp simple syrup
  • 1 tsp yuzu juice (extract)
  • ice
  • 1-2 tbsp Korean citron tea (yuja cha) (optional)
  • lime (optional; can substitute lemon)

Simple syrup:

  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup sugar

Steps:

  1. Combine club soda, shochu, simple syrup, and yuzu extract in a glass and mix well.
  2. Add ice cube and Korean citron tea (optional) and mix well.
  3. Garnish with a slice of lime, and serve immediately.

To make simple syrup:

  1. Bring the water to a boil in a small saucepan. Dissolve the sugar into the boiling water, stirring constantly.
  2. Once the sugar is dissolved completely, remove it from heat and let cool completely and thicken.
